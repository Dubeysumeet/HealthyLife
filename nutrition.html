BREAKFAST

OVERNIGHT OATS

INGREDIENTS
- Oats
- Milk (Semi-skimmed, almond or soya)
- Fruits of choice (think energy-giving bananas, brain-boosting dates and cancer-busting blueberries)
- Toppings of choice (honey and peanut butter are our faves)

INSTRUCTIONS
1. Grab a small tupperware box.
2. Spoon 4-5 tablespoons of oats in.
3. Add a half-mug of milk and stir into the mix.
4. Chop fruit and add to the mixture.
5. A dollop of honey or peanut butter, or a handful of nuts.
6. Put the lid on and leave in the fridge overnight. 8 hours, ideally.

Overview
Let’s start by keeping it simple. Grab a bag of oats (most supermarkets stock them for under Rs 100), and then chuck whatever you fancy on top. Want our advice? Mix in some honey, this'll give you an added carbohydate boost and has a lower GI rating than sugar, providing a slow, steady release of energy. Finish it off by tossing some blueberries and bananas on top. Both are great sources of carbs and contain an abundance of antioxidants, vitamins and minerals that'll help keep your body strong. Oats also contain beta-glucan, which helps stimulate your immune response.
Top tip: Unless you're fan of prison food, we recommend heating it up in the microwave, but each to his own.

MORNING SNACK

MEGA PROTEIN SHAKE

INGREDIENTS
- Almond milk, 1.25L
- Almond/peanut butter, 25g
- Banana, 2½
- Blueberries, 100g
- Oats, 75g
- Maca powder, 50g
- Protein powder, 5 servings
INSTRUCTIONS
1. Pour in the liquids first, followed by the whole ingredients, leaving the protein powder until last.
2. Once combined, blast in a blender until smooth. You can mix up the ingredients depending on your preference. Bottle each serving separately and drink within 48 hours.
Overview
The behemoth of pre-gym nutrition, you needn’t limit your shake to just powder and water. Instead, recruit these ingredients into your pre-pump palate and you’ll take your drab breakfast into the stratosphere. The almond milk and nut butters will give you a natural energy pump, while the bananas and oats boost the shake's carb levels.
Top tip: If you're really strapped for time, get all your ingredients ready in the blender the night before and blitz for 2-3 minutes in the morning and slurp it on your way to the gym. Nutritious and time-efficient.

LUNCH

VEGAN BURRITO

INGREDIENTS
-½ medium red onion, chopped
-2 garlic cloves, minced
-12 oz. extra firm tofu
-1 tbsp. olive oil
-2 large whole wheat tortillas
-Sea salt
-Pepper
INSTRUCTIONS
1. Take a medium-sized skillet, coat the surface with oil, and place it over medium heat.
2. Sauté your onions then season with salt and pepper. Afterward, throw in the garlic then fry for a minute.
3. Crumble the tofu with your hands then throw it in the pan.
4. Season with cumin, turmeric powder, salt, and pepper then stir until they combine.
5. Divide the burrito stuffing among the tortillas then roll each one.
6. Serve and enjoy.

Overview
We often wonder what the best plant-based healthy lunch ideas are. Without using any meat, is it really possible to create a bodybuilding meal? Vegan bodybuilders looking for easy protein-packed recipes can try this delicious plant-based burrito. It’s one of the best healthy food recipes for vegan athletes looking for quick lunch ideas that’ll support muscle growth. Plus, it only takes 20 minutes to cook and prepare these burritos. Bodybuilders looking for plant-based lunch ideas to replace their usual meals can try this vegan burrito recipe.

EVENING SNACK

SMOOTHIE BOWL

INGREDIENTS
- 2-3tbsp oats
- Fruit of your choice (bananas for carbohydrates and blueberries for essential vitamins and fibre)
- Optional: Handful of spinach (adds antioxidants and vitamins without compromising on taste)
- Optional: Half an avocado
- Milk, almond milk or Soya milk
- Optional: peanut butter / honey
- Ice cubes
INSTRUCTIONS
1. Drop the oats into the blender, followed by the fruit
2. Add milk and optional vegetables
4. Dollop peanut butter and honey into the mix and blend for two minutes
5. Add 3-4 ice cubes in and continue blending
6. Pour the mix into a bowl, add toppings — fruits and nuts tend to work well — and get gobbling

Overview
The best part of making a smoothie? You can whack in anything you like, within reason. Just make sure that the bulk of your smoothie is made up of oats (shock), milk, fruits (bananas for a carb boost) and ice cubes. Now it’s up to you. Go nuts – literally. They’re a great source of protein. And if you want to get a creamier texture, adding half an avocado should do the trick. Who doesn't love avocado?

DINNER

SCRAMBLED TOFU TACOS

INGREDIENTS
- 1 teaspoon olive oil
- 1 red pepper, diced
- 8 corn tortillas
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 package super firm Nasoya Tofu
- Black pepper and salt
INSTRUCTIONS
1. Place a large skillet over medium heat and coat it with oil.
2. Sauté the garlic and pepper for two minutes.
3. Afterward, crush the tofu using your hands, throw it in the pan, and then sprinkle with salt and pepper.
4. Stir and cook for five minutes while adding salt and pepper if you want to.
5. Fill the tortillas with the tofu scramble then top it off with some tomatoes, goat cheese, and avocado. You can dress it with cilantro and hot sauce if you want.
6. Serve and enjoy!

Overview
Forget that greasy, calorie-packed Mexican fast-food chain you go to for lunch. Instead, opt to pack your own healthy, delicious, guilt-free scrambled tofu tacos. This is one of the best healthy, plant-based lunch recipes both vegan and non-vegan fitness buffs can try. What makes these tofu-based lunch ideas so great is it can taste exactly how you want it to by using the right herbs and spices.
